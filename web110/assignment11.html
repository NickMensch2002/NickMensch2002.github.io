Activity- Review Mozilla: What is accessibility
This webpage on Mozilla Developer Network discusses the concept of accessibility in web development, emphasizing its significance in ensuring inclusivity and usability for all users, particularly those with disabilities. It elucidates accessibility features such as screen readers and keyboard navigation, detailing their importance in enabling people with diverse abilities to access and interact with digital content. The page also underscores the legal and ethical imperatives of adhering to accessibility standards, advocating for developers to integrate accessibility practices into their workflows from the outset of the design and development process. I can see myself using these guidelines to help better understand how to format my webpages to help those who need extra assistance with seeing, hearing etc.
